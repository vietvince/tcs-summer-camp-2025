## Getting Started with Scratch üö©

### Step 1. Remove the Default Sprite

1. In the Sprites pane, right-click the default **Cat** sprite.  
2. Select **delete** to clear the stage for your game.

### Step 2. Upload the Space Backdrop

:::info
<details style={{border:'none'}}>
    <summary>**Coach Note!**</summary>

Backdrops set the scene for your game. Choose a high-contrast space image so your sprites stand out!
</details>
:::
1. Click the **Stage** thumbnail, then the **Backdrops** tab.  
2. Click **Upload Backdrop**, select a space backdrop of your choice.  
3. Rename the backdrop to **Space Scene** by clicking its name in the Backdrops list.

### Step 3. Import the Rocketship Sprite

1. Click **Choose a Sprite** ‚Üí **Upload Sprite**.  
2. Select a rocketship of your choice.  
3. Rename this sprite to **Rocketship**.  
4. In the **Costumes** tab, center and scale it so it‚Äôs about 15‚Äì20% of the stage height.

### Step 4. Import the Laser Sprite

1. Again, **Choose a Sprite** ‚Üí **Upload Sprite**.  
2. Select a laser of your choice.  
3. Rename to **Laser**.  
4. In **Costumes**, ensure the laser points ‚Äúup‚Äù (90¬∞) so it fires correctly.

### Step 5. Import the Rock Sprite

1. **Choose a Sprite** ‚Üí **Upload Sprite**.  
2. Select a rock of your choice.  
3. Rename to **Rock**.  
4. Center the costume so its pivot point is in the middle.

### Step 6. Import the Game Over Screen

1. **Choose a Sprite** ‚Üí **Upload Sprite**.  
2. Select game over of your choice.  
3. Rename to **Game Over**.  
4. In **Costumes**, position the text in the center of the canvas.

### Step 7. Arrange and Hide

1. In the Sprites pane, verify the order (top to bottom):
   - Rocketship  
   - Laser  
   - Rock  
   - Game Over  
2. Click the **eye** icon next to **Laser**, **Rock**, and **Game Over** to hide them for now.

### Step 8. Save Your Progress

- Click **File ‚Üí Save now** to lock in your work.
