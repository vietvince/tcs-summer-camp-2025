## Objective 🧐

Train a **Pose Classification** model in Teachable Machine that can tell when you “duck” to dodge an incoming meteor image.

## Getting Started with Pose Project 🚩

### Step 1. Create a New Pose Project  
1. Go to [Teachable Machine](https://teachablemachine.withgoogle.com).  
2. Click **Get Started** → **Pose Project** → **Standard Pose Model**.

### Step 2. Rename Your Classes  
1. Click **Class 1** and rename it **Standing**.  
2. Click **Class 2** and rename it **Ducking**.

### Step 3. Record Training Examples  
1. Select **Standing**, click **Webcam** → **Start**, and record ~20 examples of you standing tall (arms at sides).  
2. Select **Ducking**, record ~20 examples of you crouching or covering your head.

### Step 4. Train Your Model  
1. Click **Train Model** at the bottom.  
2. Wait for the progress bar to finish—this takes just a minute or two.

### Step 5. Test Your Meteor Dodger  
1. Switch to the **Preview** tab.  
2. Hold up a printed meteor image (or a drawing) to the camera while standing—observe the prediction.  
3. Then duck—check that the model now labels you “Ducking.”

### Step 6. Export & Share  
1. Click **Export Model** → **Download** (TensorFlow.js).  
2. Use the exported files in a simple webpage to show a meteor graphic and display “DODGED!” when your pose is detected.

<br />

- [x] **Great job!** You’ve trained your **Meteor Dodger** model.
